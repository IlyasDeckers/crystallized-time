# Quantum mechanics in music

Quantum mechanics in music involves both the mathematical parallels between wave functions and sound, and the deliberate application of quantum concepts—like superposition and entanglement—to compositional structure. It explores how music can exist in multiple states simultaneously and uses quantum technology to create new soundscapes.

> "The laws of nature are what we explore for generating music. We're not using a classical system that takes notions from these rules. We need to make a system that are the rules." 

### Musical Superposition

Similar to how particles exist in multiple states until observed, musical chords and complex soundscapes can represent a "superposition" of sounds. In quantum music, this is achieved by creating pieces that resonate differently and feel unpredictable until heard, shifting in form with each experience.

### Harmonics and Quantum Waves

The physical principles of quantum mechanics, such as energy existing in discrete packets, are analogous to the quantized nature of musical notes and harmonics. Electrons in an atom, for instance, behave as harmonic oscillators, with their energy fields organizing into patterns of ripples that resemble music intervals.

### Compositional Techniques

Quantum Concept Music (QCM), use quantum entanglement and measurement principles to structure compositions, allowing musicians to interact based on Bell-pair measurements.
